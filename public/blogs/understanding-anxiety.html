Understanding Anxiety: A Gentle Guide

The Mindful Heart · April 2026

Anxiety is one of the most common emotional experiences — and one of the most misunderstood. It is not simply "worrying too much." It is a whole-body response that can affect how we think, feel, sleep, and relate to others.

What anxiety feels like

For some, anxiety shows up as racing thoughts or a sense of dread. For others, it appears as restlessness, muscle tension, stomach discomfort, or difficulty concentrating. All of these are valid ways the nervous system signals that something feels unsafe or overwhelming.

A compassionate starting point

Rather than fighting anxiety, therapy often begins by understanding what it is trying to protect. When we meet anxiety with curiosity instead of criticism, we create room for meaningful change.

When to reach out

If anxiety is interfering with your daily life, relationships, or sense of wellbeing, speaking with a therapist can help. You do not need to have it all figured out before reaching out — that first step is often the hardest and the most important.
